Arroz Caldo

Description

Loaded with ginger flavor, hearty, tasty and the ultimate comfort food. Delicious as a midday snack, light meal and a go-to for breakfast

Backstory

Arroz Caldo, commonly confused with 'Lugaw' is a Filipino rice based dish. Both are made with chicken and ginger. The key difference is the consistency. Lugaw is more porridge-like, while Arroz Caldo is soupier. Arroz Caldo is usually served with a side of boiled egg and green onions, while Lugaw is not.

It is served as comfort food in Philippine culture and is a popular breakfast meal. The name is derived from Spanish 'arroz', meaning rice and 'caldo', meaning broth.

Despite its Spanish name, it has origins from the congee introduced by Chinese-Filipino migrants, diverging over centuries to use Filipino ingredients and to suit the local tastes.

Ingredients

  • 1 (1.4-1.8kg) whole chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 onion, peeled and chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 4 tablespoons fresh ginger, peeled and minced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 cup uncooked rice
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 broth cubes
  • salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings

  • 3 hardboiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 1/4 cup fried garlic bits
  • 1/4 green onions, chopped
  • calamansi or lemon, cut into wedges
  • fish sauce

Steps

  1. Trim chicken of unwanted fat, rinse and drain well
  2. In a pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions, ginger and garlic. Cook, stirring regularly, until softened and aromatic
  3. Add chicken and cook, sitting constantly, until lightly browned and have rendered juices. Add fish sauce and continune to cook for around 1 to 2 minutes.
  4. Add rice and cook, stirring regularly, for about 1 to 2 minutes or until rice starts to lightly brown
  5. Add water and bring to a boil, skimming scum that floats on top.
  6. Add broth cubes and stir until dissolved
  7. Lower heatm cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice has softened and expanded and the congee has thickened to desired consistency.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Ladle into serving bowls. Top with boiled egg, green onions, and toasted garlic. Serve hot with calamansi and additional fish sauce on the side.
